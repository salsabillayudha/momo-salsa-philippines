LEG 01

Palawan

DAY 1 – 12 · Manila → Puerto Princesa → Balabac → El Nido → Coron

The heavy-logistics leg comes first, while energy is high. Balabac is the wildest place on this trip — the reward is water that doesn't look real.

DAY 01Meet in ManilaDXB → MNLCGK → MNL

Two planes, one arrivals hall. The trip officially starts with a hug.

  • MorningLand at NAIA — Momo from Dubai, Salsa from Jakarta. Find each other at arrivals.
  • MiddayDrop bags in Makati. First Jollibee of the trip — Chickenjoy + spaghetti, obviously.
  • EveningPoblacion stroll — speakeasies & rooftop bars, if jetlag allows.
  • NightEarly-ish sleep. Domestic flight tomorrow.
DAY 02Fly to Puerto PrincesaMNL → PPS

Gateway to Palawan. Stock up today — Balabac has nothing.

  • MorningFlight MNL → PPS (~1h 20m). Check in, quick lunch.
  • AfternoonBuy expedition supplies: reef-safe sunblock, dry bags, power banks. Withdraw plenty of pesos.
  • EveningBaywalk sunset stroll along the water.
  • NightSeafood feast at Kalui (book ahead). Pack light for Balabac.
DAY 03 – 06

Balabac Expedition · 4D3N

The crown jewel. The edge of the Philippines: Onuk Island and its iconic sandbar, Punta Sebaring, Candaraman, Patawan. Basic homestays, almost no signal, unreal water.

  • Pre-dawnPickup, ~5h overland to Rio Tuba, then boats out to the islands.
  • DaysSandbars, snorkeling untouched reefs, island-to-island by boat.
  • NightsHomestay dinners, stargazing with zero light pollution, glowing plankton if you're lucky. Fully offline.

After Dark

Nights out

Honest expectation-setting: only Boracay has a real party scene. Everywhere else is sunset drinks and live music — which, frankly, is the point. Energy meter: ★★★★★ = dance until 3am, ★ = one beautiful beer.

Boracay — the big nights ★★★★★

DAY 14 – 15 · STATION 1 & 2

Happy hours run long (some buy-1-get-1 from 4pm), bars go until 1–3am. Start at a Station 1 sunset bar, end wherever the night takes you.

  • Epic — the island's flagship beach club, next to D'Mall; dinner spot that turns nightclub after midnight
  • OM Bar — Epic's neighbor and rival, dance floor on the sand, hookah upstairs
  • Exit Bar — beach-bungalow vibe, cheap drinks, sandy dance floor; arrive before midnight
  • Prisma at Station X — the stylish one: the country's biggest rum collection, proper cocktails
  • BIBCO — craft brewery; try the Kiamoy Sour and Hazy Ube
  • Boracay PubCrawl — if you two feel like collecting new friends for a night
Manila — Poblacion ★★★★★

DAY 1 · IF JETLAG PERMITS

Makati's Poblacion district: speakeasies, rooftop bars, natural wine — all walking distance. A very good first date night before the islands.

El Nido ★★★★★

DAY 8 – 9

Pukka Bar is the legend — live reggae right on the beach. Small scene, big charm, done by midnight because tomorrow is lagoon day.

Coron ★★★★★

DAY 10 – 12

Sleepy town, 8am boat departures. Your best night here is the hot spring under the stars, not a bar — and that's a feature.

Panglao — Alona ★★★★★

DAY 16 – 18

The Alona strip after dark: beach bars, acoustic sets, fire dancers, happy hours everywhere. Relaxed with a pulse.

Siquijor ★★★★★

DAY 19 – 20

One name: Baha Bar. Live music in a garden, island-mystic energy. Not a party — often the most memorable night anyway.

Good to know

Visas

Indonesian passport: visa-free 30 days. Momo: check requirements for your passport nationality — UAE residency alone doesn't grant entry, the passport does.

Weather

December = amihan season. Mostly dry and sunny, but northern Palawan seas can get choppy — tours occasionally reschedule. Balabac is at its best.

Cash

Balabac and Siquijor are cash territory, and Coron ATMs run dry in peak season. Load up on pesos in Puerto Princesa and Cebu.

Peak season

Christmas/New Year weeks can double prices. Book the big three early: Balabac tour, El Nido–Coron ferry, Boracay hotel.

Connectivity

Grab a local eSIM or Globe/Smart SIM at MNL. Expect near-zero signal in Balabac — tell people in advance.

Packing

Reef-safe sunscreen, dry bag, water shoes, motion-sickness tabs for ferries, one warm layer for night boats and cold planes.
